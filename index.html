Representational Equity Index (REI) by District
Reading the REI

REI = District Black % minus statewide Black % (31.2%). Positive = packed — Black voters over-concentrated, votes above the majority threshold are structurally wasted. Negative = cracked — Black voters dispersed, cannot reach decisive electoral share. Zero would be proportional. Under Act 2 (SB 121), District 2 is the only district where the SOS registration data shows Black voters at or above a level that could support electoral influence. The REI is computed from SOS registration data (31.2% statewide Black registration share as baseline), not from Census PL 94-171 VAP.

SOS Registration Data -- Boundary Reconciliation Required

The Louisiana Secretary of State voter registration file (5/1/2026) used in this dashboard was not reported by the SOS using Act 2 congressional district boundaries. District-level registration figures in this dashboard are derived by assigning whole-parish SOS data to districts based on the Act 2 parish assignment scheme. This is an analytical approximation only.

Unless and until the SOS explicitly reports registration data by Act 2 district geography, or registration data is spatially reassigned to Act 2 census blocks, all district-level registration comparisons in this dashboard are provisional and must not be treated as final Act 2 district statistics. This applies to all charts, district cards, REI deviations, and registration percentages shown under any district label.

Census PL 94-171 VAP data (a separate, independent source) confirms District 2 as the only majority Black VAP district (58.24% Black VAP per the district demographics file). District 6 Black VAP is 24.58% -- not majority Black. SOS registration figures and PL 94-171 VAP figures are not equivalent and are not compared against each other in this dashboard.

14th Amendment Review -- One-Person-One-Vote

Plaintiffs do not need a VRA claim to challenge Act 2. The Equal Protection Clause requires mathematical population equality among congressional districts. The anomalies documented below were identified in the SB 121 Reengrossed text (pre-enrollment) using the legislature's own official tables. Whether they persist in the enrolled Act 2 text requires verification against the enrolled document. If confirmed, they represent potential malapportionment on the face of the legislation.

Beaullieu House Floor Amendment — Legislative Process Record

House Floor Amendment HFASB121 3645 5695, authored by Rep. Beaullieu, replaced the prior House Committee amendments (#5999) in their entirety with a complete precinct-level rewrite of all six congressional districts. The amendment was adopted 10-7 on May 21, 2026 during the floor session — not in committee. Parishes affected by the precinct reassignments include Morehouse, Lincoln, Jackson, Grant, Calcasieu, St. Landry, Tangipahoa, Pointe Coupee, and others across all six districts. Each precinct transfer may have introduced split-line mismatches between the static "2026 Precinct Shapefiles (1-27-2026)" and the live SOS voter registry. Per Rep. Rodney Lyons (D-Marrero), per the May 21, 2026 floor record: the body was "performing surgery on maps with no input." The Beaullieu amendment text is fully incorporated into enrolled Act 2.

14th Amendment — One Person, One Vote Challenge

Why This Matters — No VRA Claim Required

The Equal Protection Clause requires mathematical population equality among congressional districts regardless of any Voting Rights Act claim. Post-Callais, this is the primary remaining federal challenge pathway. The legislature's own tables — entered into the official record — show facially impossible registration rates in multiple District 1 parishes. If confirmed in the enrolled Act 2 text, these anomalies constitute a one-person, one-vote challenge on the face of the legislation.

Controlling Legal Authority

Reynolds v. Sims, 377 U.S. 533 (1964): Equal Protection requires that legislative districts be substantially equal in population. The one-person, one-vote doctrine applies with full force to congressional districting.

Wesberry v. Sanders, 376 U.S. 1 (1964): Article I, § 2 requires that congressional districts be as equal in population as practicable.

Evenwel v. Abbott, 578 U.S. 54 (2016): States may use total population as the apportionment base. The anomalies documented here are internal contradictions within the legislature's own declared VAP figures — the bill's stated parish-level VAP totals do not sum to the bill's stated district VAP, and six parishes show registered voter counts exceeding their stated VAP ceiling.

District 1 Internal VAP Contradiction — 124,280 Phantom Residents

Per SB 121 Reengrossed · Page 11: The sum of individual parish VAP figures for District 1 equals 477,567. The bill's district-level summary declares D1 VAP = 601,847. The gap of 124,280 people does not appear in any Louisiana population database and suggests a systemic column misalignment in the shapefile-to-bill conversion process. This is an internal mathematical contradiction within the legislature's own document.

Under Reynolds v. Sims and its progeny, a district map whose population figures contain a 124,280-person internal discrepancy cannot satisfy the one-person, one-vote requirement. This is not an external challenge to the legislature's methodology — it is a challenge to the legislature's own arithmetic.

Next Steps for Litigation Teams

1. Verify enrollment: Pull the enrolled Act 2 statutory text from LRS 18:1276 and confirm whether the VAP figures on Pages 11–12 of the reengrossed text persist. If they do, you have a facially invalid map.

2. Commission independent population analysis: Use U.S. Census PL 94-171 block-level data (available in this repo as la_pl2020_official/) joined to Act 2 district boundaries (SB_121_Enrolled.shp) to produce certified district population totals independent of the legislature's tables.

3. Calculate deviation: Compute the maximum population deviation across the six districts using Census VAP. Any deviation exceeding 1% in a congressional map, or any deviation traceable to intentional racial sorting, triggers strict scrutiny under Karcher v. Daggett, 462 U.S. 725 (1983).

Data Source Hierarchy
Sources Listed in Order of Legal Authority

The following hierarchy governs how conflicting data should be resolved. Sources higher on the list carry greater legal weight.

  1. Act 2 Block Equivalency and Geometry (Enrolled SB 121) -- The official legal definition of the six congressional districts enacted under R.S. 18:1276. This is the authoritative source for district boundaries. All geographic analysis in this dashboard uses the Act 2 shapefile derived from the enrolled boundary definition.
  2. U.S. Census Bureau 2020 P.L. 94-171 Population and VAP Data -- Block-level population counts and Voting Age Population figures derived from the decennial Census. This is an official population source and is legally and conceptually distinct from voter registration data. VAP measures all residents aged 18 and older regardless of citizenship or registration status. The district demographics file confirms District 2 as the only majority Black VAP district (58.24% Black VAP). District 6 Black VAP is 24.58% -- not majority Black.
  3. VTD Assignment Outputs -- Precinct-to-district assignment tables produced during the mapping process. These link Census blocks and precincts to Act 2 districts and are used to roll up block-level population data to district geography.
  4. Louisiana Secretary of State Active Voter Registration File (5/1/2026) -- Statewide registration counts by party and race as of May 1, 2026. This file reflects voter registration at the time of data extraction. It was not reported by the SOS using Act 2 district geography. District-level registration figures in this dashboard are derived by applying the Act 2 parish assignment to whole-parish SOS data. This is an analytical approximation, not an official SOS Act 2 district report. All district-level registration statistics in this dashboard are provisional.
  5. Derived Analysis -- All computed ratios, REI deviations, PACKED/CRACKED classifications, partisan lean indicators, and narrative interpretations are analytical constructs produced by this dashboard. They are not official government determinations. The REI baseline (31.2%) is the Black share of SOS registered voters statewide -- it is a registration figure, not a Census VAP figure.
VAP vs. Registration -- Critical Distinction

Census PL 94-171 VAP and SOS voter registration data measure different things. VAP counts all residents 18 and older. Registration counts only those who have completed the voter registration process. These datasets produce different racial composition percentages for the same district. This dashboard does not equate registered voters with VAP and does not claim that registration counts match PL 94-171 VAP counts. Charts and narrative labeled "SOS" reflect registration data only.
